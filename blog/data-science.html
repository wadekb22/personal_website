Why am I learning data science?

May 3, 2020

It seems funny to me looking back, but I can remember the exact moment I realized I should study data science. I was giving a tour the fall of my senior year to a set of prospective engineering students. While I was summarizing my own interests in image processing historic manuscripts, a father noted that data science might be applicable.

At this point I’d already had a taste for what data science entailed. The summer prior I helped design a machine learning foundation for a CRM tool . But I’d never really considered how universal those skills could be. As I thought about it, I realized how crucial data is to everything. It lets us find bigger patterns in the world that would otherwise go unexplored.

In some ways, I feel like an adventurer as I consider the effects of data science. No one before would have been able to find conclusions in the vast amount of information we have to work with today. But if I can make sense of the chaos, I’ll be able to see worlds of understanding that no one else has before. And like those early pioneers, these discoveries will pave the way for society to move forward into a new world.
